    How did he get his stage name?

    He named his alter ego after an English village while driving back from a gig in the late 1980s. He claims to be the only comedian in the world named after a Lincolnshire village.

    He often incorporates guitar playing into his act, featuring oddball and slightly twisted songs. He is known for his irrelevant song titles, such as Planet Dog and Wot Italian, which often do not reflect the actual content of the song.

    He moved away from stand-up because it stopped making him laugh, especially when considering the political situation and the general feeling in the country at the time. Instead, he focused on writing and performing music.

    Among his albums are Wot Italian?, His & the Following People (a live recording), Songs For Dogs, Funerals….., and Bang! Is This Your Vehicle, Sir?. His music features contributions from artists such as Nick Pynn, Kevin Hearn, and Omid Djalili.

    Has he written any stage plays?

    Yes, he has written several stage plays including The Condition of the Virgin, One Night Stand: God and Adam, and Hitler Sells Tickets, all of which were adapted for BBC Radio.
