    Meryl Streep, Daughter Mamie Gummer Rock in Ricki and the Flash Clip

    For Meryl Streep and her eldest daughter Mamie Gummer, the family business is show business. The two actresses star together in the upcoming dramedy Ricki and the Flash, taking inspiration from their own relationship to play an on-screen mother-daughter pair.

    02 Apr 2024, 00:00

    For Meryl Streep and her eldest daughter Mamie Gummer, the family business is show business. The two actresses star together in the upcoming dramedy Ricki and the Flash, taking inspiration from their own relationship to play an on-screen mother-daughter pair.

    The first trailer for the flick made its way online on Wednesday, May 6, showing Streep, 65, alongside Gummer, 31. This will be the first feature film that gives Gummer a starring role with her mother, while she has made appearances in the Oscar winner’s movies before, including cameos in 1986’s Heartburn, when she was just 2 years old, and 2007’s Evening.

    Streep transformed for the umpteenth time for Ricki and the Flash, letting her hair down to play a veteran rocker named Ricki, who has spent more time over the years with her band than her family. She reunites with her daughter Julie (Gummer), as well as her ex-husband Pete (Kevin Kline) and his new wife Maureen (Audra McDonald), and son Joshua (Sebastian Stan), as the family goes through some inner turmoil.

    Absent from her kids’ lives for years, Ricki must reconcile her damaged relationships with the two as she attempts to reconnect with them. Penned by Diablo Cody (Juno) and under the direction of Jonathan Demme (Philadelphia), Ricki and the Flash also stars Ben Platt, Rick Springfield, and Charlotte Rae, among others. 

    Streep — who previously sang in Into the Woods, Mammi Mia!, and Postcards from the Edge — shows off some impressive singing chops in the first look, whipping her long, braided hair around as she sings Dobie Gray’s 1970s classic “Drift Away.” The celebrated actress also tackles intensely emotional scenes as well, including one in which she is shown breaking down crying during one particularly tough family moment.
